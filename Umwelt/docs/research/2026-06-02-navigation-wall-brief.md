# The Wall in the Sky: Heading, Path Integration, and the Limits of a Graded Non-Spiking Substrate — A Design Brief and Landscape for *Umwelt*

## TL;DR
- **Ship raw polarization channels and keep the wall standing.** Instantaneous, coarse heading *readout* from a few sinusoidally-tuned e-vector channels IS honestly buildable feedforward (population-vector / divisive-normalization / winner-take-all), and a *discrete* heading hold is buildable from mutual inhibition (flip-flop / few-state attractor). What is NOT honestly buildable from your 5 primitives is the **continuous, drift-free heading estimate that integrates angular velocity through darkness** — the ring attractor — because it requires precisely fine-tuned recurrent excitation whose mathematical signature (a continuum of marginally-stable, zero-eigenvalue fixed points) is structurally unstable and cannot be assembled from discrete bistable elements without collapsing into a few drifting attractor states.
- **F-PI should be "PI with forgetting" (leaky), and this is honest, not a compromise.** In the canonical bee model (Stone et al., 2017), the CPU4 home-vector "memory" is itself a *leaky* integrator (explicit uniform memory-loss term k = 0.1), the CPU1 steering stage is a pure feedforward bilateral difference, and the only genuinely hard recurrent core is the TB1 heading ring attractor. Desert-ant behavior (Müller & Wehner, 1994) confirms biological PI accumulates error and "runs out," triggering systematic search — so leaky PI is closer to reality than perfect PI.
- **The feedforward-friendly big win is view-based homing.** Mushroom-body familiarity (Ardin et al., 2016) is a feedforward sparse-coding layer (20,000 Kenyon cells → one output neuron) plus a single dopamine-gated depressing plasticity rule that maps directly onto your sensor → inter → modulator-gated-edge → motor primitives, with no attractor required. Make view-homing the buildable triumph and the ring attractor the deliberate, well-motivated "wall."

## A. DECISION-READY BRIEF

### A1. F-COMPASS: ship raw polarization, and here is exactly where the wall falls

**Position: Ship only raw, sinusoidally-tuned polarization-opponent channels (the honest DRA hardware).** This is faithful to biology. In real insects the dorsal rim area (DRA) photoreceptors are intrinsically polarization-sensitive, with orthogonal microvillar analyzers (in a locust DRA ommatidium, photoreceptor 7's microvilli are perpendicular to those of receptors 1, 2, 5, 6, 8), and downstream POL-neurons are e-vector–opponent: each is maximally excited at one e-vector angle (Φmax) and maximally inhibited at the orthogonal angle, giving a sinusoidal tuning curve with a ~90° period structure. In crickets, Sakura, Lambrinos & Labhart (2008, *J Neurophysiol* 100:469–480) resolved from 142 POL1-neuron recordings exactly **three physiological POL1 types tuned to e-vectors oriented ∼10°, 60°, and 130°** to the long axis of the head. So the "honest" sensor is a handful of channels whose graded 0–1 output is a sinusoidal function of e-vector orientation — exactly a raw transduced signal, not a computed heading.

**(1) Is instantaneous heading decoding feedforward-buildable from these primitives? YES — and this should be a buildable puzzle, not part of the wall.** The decode is a population-vector / sinusoidal-array sum. Gkanias et al. (2019, *PLOS Comput Biol* 15:e1007123) showed an array of polarization-opponent channels can directly estimate solar azimuth: the population response forms a sinusoid whose **phase** encodes azimuth and whose **amplitude** encodes confidence (they decode it with an FFT, but the operation is fundamentally "sum of sinusoidally-weighted channels," i.e. a weighted convergence onto two opponent neurons — a sine-like and cosine-like projection). Stone et al.'s own TL→CL1→TB1 front end is exactly this: a feedforward mapping from sinusoidally-tuned compass inputs to a sinusoidal population code. In your primitives:
- Sensor channels (graded, sinusoidal in e-vector) → excitatory and inhibitory interneurons performing **opponency** (a difference of two orthogonally-tuned channels = a signed sinusoid).
- A small bank of such opponent units with different preferred angles = a population code of heading.
- A **winner-take-all** (mutual inhibition among the bank) or **divisive normalization** (a pooling inhibitory interneuron that scales the bank) yields a coarse heading readout.

All of this is feedforward + lateral inhibition + magnitude operations — squarely inside the "magnitude + slow-dynamics" regime. **Verdict: instantaneous coarse heading decode is buildable and should be a player-solvable challenge.**

**Two honest caveats the game should respect:**
- **180° (e-vector) ambiguity.** A single e-vector orientation is defined only mod 180°, so polarization alone gives an axis, not a direction. Gkanias et al. solve the solar/antisolar-meridian ambiguity using the *degree* of polarization across the sky and a biologically constrained sensor array. The game can either (a) supply a second cue channel (intensity/chromatic gradient — biologically, the anterior optic tubercle integrates UV/green sky contrast with polarization), or (b) make resolving the ambiguity a puzzle.
- **Time-compensation (ephemeris).** A true allocentric compass must compensate for the sun moving across the sky. This needs either a clock input or the confidence-based trick Gkanias et al. propose. This is legitimately advanced content.

**(2) Where exactly does the discrete-vs-continuous wall fall?** This is the crux. There are three qualitatively different "heading" behaviors, and the wall runs between the second and third:

| Capability | Substrate needed | Buildable in *Umwelt*? |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Instantaneous heading readout** (decode current e-vector while cue is present) | Feedforward opponency + WTA/normalization | **YES** — feedforward |
| **(b) Discrete/coarse heading HOLD** (latch one of N sectors; persists briefly without input) | Mutual inhibition → flip-flop / few-state discrete attractor | **YES** — but only N discrete states, with switching/jitter |
| **(c) Continuous, drift-free heading that integrates angular velocity through darkness** | Ring attractor: fine-tuned recurrent excitation + surround/global inhibition on a ring topology | **NO** — this is the wall |

Mutual inhibition between two graded neurons gives you a flip-flop / bistable latch; rings of such latches give you a few discrete stable states. This is genuinely useful and honest — it can hold a coarse heading sector for a while. But it is categorically different from a continuous attractor. **The wall falls precisely at the transition from "holding one of a small number of discrete heading sectors" to "holding and smoothly updating a heading that can take any value on the circle and is advanced by integrating turn rate."** See B2 for the mathematical reason.

### A2. F-PI: decompose Stone et al. (2017) and decide on leaky PI

**The circuit, decomposed by buildability** (all signals graded 0–1; the model is explicitly rate-based, with weights ∈ {−1, 0, +1} and sigmoid activation — strikingly close to your primitives):

- **TL → CL1 → TB1 (heading):** The front end is feedforward (buildable). But **TB1** is the heading representation and is implemented as a **ring attractor** via mutual inhibition among the 8 TB1 cells "whose synaptic weights increase with distance of each TB-neuron pair." *This is the hard recurrent core.* Stone et al. literally annotate it: "This layer thus acts as a ring attractor."
- **TN (speed):** Optic-flow speed neurons — raw transduced graded signal. Buildable (it is a sensor).
- **CPU4 (memory / accumulator):** A row of **leaky integrators**, one per heading column, each summing speed gated by heading. **Buildable from your primitives IF you can supply heading + speed** — it is leaky integration, which is exactly a primitive dynamic you allow. Critically, the update rule has accumulation rate h = 0.0025 **and a uniform memory-loss (leak) rate k = 0.1**. So even the canonical model's "memory" *decays*.
- **Pontine cells:** inhibitory normalizers balancing left/right CPU4 sums — buildable (inhibitory interneurons).
- **CPU1 (steering):** A **feedforward bilateral difference** — left vs right summed activity drives turn direction. This is the "compare current heading to desired heading" operation and is the most clearly buildable, honest part. Stone et al. (2017, *Current Biology* 27:3069–3085) state that "the steering principle we have described, a consequence of the CPU4-CPU1 columnar offset, could be a general purpose mechanism by which the CX compares an animal's current heading to its desired heading to generate an appropriate correction" — and suggest this steering-by-comparison is plausibly the *ancestral* core function from which path integration evolved.

So: **the buildable parts are the CPU4 leaky accumulator bank and the CPU1 bilateral-difference steering; the un-buildable part is the TB1 ring-attractor heading representation** (plus the persistent, non-decaying ideal of memory, which the biology doesn't actually deliver anyway).

**(2) Is CPU4 a true persistent accumulator or leaky? Leaky — and leakiness is biologically real.** In the Stone model the memory has an explicit decay term (k = 0.1). More importantly, *behavior* confirms leaky/error-accumulating PI: Müller & Wehner (1994, *J Comp Physiol A* 175:525–530) — "the hidden spiral" — showed *Cataglyphis fortis* that has run off its home vector but not found the nest performs systematic search in ever-increasing loops centred on the PI-estimated origin; and Merkle, Knaden & Wehner (2006, *J Exp Biol* 209:3545–3549) showed the *width* of the search scales with the length of the preceding foraging run, i.e. the ants behave as though their confidence in the integrator degrades with accumulated distance. Sommer & Wehner (2004) found ants systematically *underestimate* distance, with error growing with travel distance (best fit logarithmic/exponential). **Verdict: "PI with forgetting" is not a concession to your engine's limits — it is closer to the biological reality than perfect PI would be.** Label it honestly as leaky and make the "vector runs out → systematic search" behavior an emergent feature, not a bug.

**(3) Minimal recurrence for a continuous attractor, and honest approximations.** The minimal continuous (ring) attractor needs recurrent connectivity precisely tuned so that activity has a continuum of marginally-stable states (see B2). Honest approximations available in your substrate:
- **Discrete ring of N flip-flops / bistable units:** gives N discrete heading states. Documented failure modes: (i) **discretization error** (heading quantized to 360°/N); (ii) **drift toward the nearest discrete attractor** when the true heading lies between states; (iii) **switching/jumps** rather than smooth motion; (iv) inability to integrate small/slow angular velocities (sub-threshold turns are lost). Counterintuitively, coarser (fewer-state) versions can be *more* robust to noise because the energy barriers between discrete states resist drift (see B2/B5).
- **Leaky integrator bank (CPU4-style) without a true heading attractor:** works while the heading cue is present (feedforward decode feeds the accumulators), but cannot maintain heading in darkness — exactly the biological dependency on the celestial cue.

**Is a restricted recurrent affordance worth it? Recommendation: NO — let the wall stand as deliberate content, with ONE optional, clearly-bounded exception.** The cleanest design is to let players build (a) feedforward instantaneous heading, (b) discrete heading holds from mutual inhibition, and (c) leaky PI that depends on a live compass cue — and to make the *failure* of the discrete approximation (drift, quantization, loss in darkness) the teaching moment about why real insects evolved a ring attractor. If you want a single advanced affordance, the most honest one is **not** a "ring attractor primitive" but the **velocity-shift mechanism** itself exposed as a buildable challenge: in flies, two cell types (E-PG compass cells and P-EN cells conjunctively tuned to heading and angular velocity) implement a "copy-and-shift" where asymmetric, turn-direction-dependent input nudges the activity bump around the ring (Green et al., 2017; Turner-Evans et al., 2017). Even then, the *substrate that holds the bump* still needs the fine-tuned recurrence — so exposing the shift mechanism without a stable bump just produces a fancier drifting estimate. That is arguably the most honest "almost works" content you can ship.

## B. LANDSCAPE / DEEPER UNDERSTANDING

### B1. Taxonomy of insect navigation strategies and their buildability

| Strategy | Known/hypothesized circuit | Feedforward vs recurrent | Honestly buildable from graded non-spiking primitives? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beacon/landmark taxis (tropotaxis)** | Bilateral sensor comparison → steering; maps onto CX PFL3 unilateral steering subsets | Pure feedforward (left−right difference) | **Fully** — the canonical Braitenberg vehicle; a sensor→inter→motor difference |
| **Klinotaxis** | Sequential sampling + comparison across time (needs a one-sample memory) | Feedforward + short memory (leaky integrator) | **Yes** — needs one leaky-integrator "previous sample" store |
| **Menotaxis (fixed bearing)** | Hold a desired heading offset vs a celestial cue; CX compass + steering | Feedforward steering + a *held* desired heading | **Partially** — coarse/discrete desired heading buildable; precise arbitrary bearing held in darkness needs the attractor |
| **Route following / view-based** | Mushroom body familiarity (KC sparse code → MBON), scan for least-novel direction | Feedforward + one plastic layer | **Yes — the big win** (see B4) |
| **View-based homing (snapshot)** | rIDF/rotational image matching; MB familiarity gradient | Feedforward image comparison | **Yes** — image-difference minimum = steering signal |
| **Path integration** | CX: TB1 ring attractor + CPU4 accumulator + CPU1 steering | Feedforward accumulator + steering, **recurrent heading core** | **Partially** — leaky PI buildable given a compass; drift-free heading is the wall |
| **Systematic search** | Spiral/loop generator centred on PI origin; emerges when vector runs out | Oscillatory / feedforward with leaky state | **Yes** — half-center oscillator (mutual inhibition) gives loop generation |

The pattern is clear: **everything that is feedforward + leaky integration + mutual inhibition is buildable; the single capability that is not is the continuous attractor at the heart of allocentric heading.** Menotaxis and PI both inherit the wall *only* through their dependence on a persistent continuous heading.

### B2. Ring attractors / CANs in depth — and the mathematical reason the wall exists

A continuous attractor network (CAN) represents a continuous variable (heading) as a localized "bump" of activity that can sit anywhere on a ring of neurons. Its defining properties (Seelig & Jayaraman, 2015, *Nature* 521:186–191, in the *Drosophila* ellipsoid body): (1) tracks an external cue; (2) **persists** when the cue is removed; (3) locks to one of two competing cues; (4) occasionally switches stochastically; (5) moves smoothly when the cue moves. The fly head-direction system (E-PG "compass" neurons in the EB, arranged in a physical ring; updated by P-EN cells) is the textbook biological CAN (Seelig & Jayaraman, 2015; Kim et al., 2017; Green et al., 2017; Turner-Evans et al., 2017, 2020). **[All fly, not ant.]**

**What a CAN structurally requires:** local recurrent excitation (nearby ring positions excite each other) + global/surround inhibition (a "Mexican-hat" or inhibition-dominated kernel), on a ring topology. Formally, the network must have a **continuum of fixed points forming a ring-shaped attractor manifold**, where perturbations *along* the manifold are neither amplified nor damped (neutral/marginal stability, **zero eigenvalue**) while perturbations *off* the manifold decay quickly (negative eigenvalues).

**Why this cannot be honestly assembled from discrete bistable elements — the fine-tuning problem.** This is the deep answer to "why does the wall exist computationally."
- **A true continuum of marginally-stable states is structurally unstable (non-generic).** Seung (1996, *PNAS* 93:13339–13344, "How the brain keeps the eyes still") established that line/continuous attractor dynamics require **precisely tuned positive feedback**; generic (untuned) perturbations break the continuum into either a single point attractor (everything drifts back to one state) or no fixed point at all (unidirectional drift). The continuum only exists on a measure-zero tuning surface. As the CAN literature puts it: "Exact continuous attractors are structurally unstable — generic perturbations reduce the continuum to a slow manifold populated by discrete fixed points or cycles."
- **Zhang (1996, *J Neurosci* 16:2112–2126)** gave the analytic decomposition: an **even-symmetric** weight component sustains the bump (the continuum), and an **odd-symmetric** component shifts it at a velocity-controlled rate. Both must be tuned; the asymmetric component's strength must precisely match angular velocity to achieve gain-1 integration.
- **The handoff requires a zero eigenvalue everywhere.** Noorman, Hulse, Jayaraman, Romani & Hermundstad (2024, *Nature Neuroscience* 27:2207–2217, "Maintaining and updating accurate internal representations of continuous variables with a handful of neurons"; preprint 2022, *bioRxiv*, "Accurate angular integration with only a handful of neurons") show a ring attractor is "a discrete set of N line attractors … stitched together," and "achieving this precise handoff requires precise tuning, such that the leading eigenvalue λ of all active submatrices of W is zero." Without that zero eigenvalue, "the energy landscape is bumpy, with discrete minima separated by barriers," and the bump "drift[s] toward the set of discrete orientations corresponding to the local minima." Stable and unstable fixed points end up spaced by π/N radians.

**This is the mathematical wall.** A flip-flop / discrete bistable element is, by construction, a system with a *bumpy* energy landscape and discrete minima separated by barriers — the opposite of the *flat* landscape a continuous attractor needs. You can chain N of them into a discrete ring, but you get exactly the failure modes the math predicts: heading quantized to 360°/N, drift to the nearest minimum, jumps instead of smooth motion, and inability to integrate sub-barrier angular velocities. You cannot flatten the landscape into a true continuum without precisely tuned recurrent *excitation* that balances inhibition to give a zero eigenvalue — and "fine-tuned recurrent excitation with a zero-eigenvalue balance" is precisely what a palette of discrete primitives, mutual inhibition, and leaky integration cannot guarantee or hold.

**How accuracy/drift scale with N (the surprising part).** Noorman et al. (2024) show that within a fixed connectivity scheme, **noise robustness (inverse diffusion) increases linearly with network size N**, tuning tolerance scales linearly with N, and the desirable-parameter-space volume grows at least quadratically with N — so *bigger* tuned networks are easier to keep accurate. Strikingly, networks "even with as few as 4 neurons" can "achieve the performance of much larger networks" (3 suffice for a minimal line attractor), but small optimal networks require more fine-tuning and are less robust to noise. Conversely — and this is the counterintuitive lesson for your design — a *coarse, untuned, discrete* ring can be **more** robust to noise-driven drift precisely because its energy barriers resist diffusion (a "washboard potential" slows the bump's random walk). Pisokas, Heinze & Webb (2020, *eLife* 9:e53985, "The head direction circuit of two insect species") demonstrate this biologically: "differences in the inhibition pattern enable the fruit fly circuit to respond faster to heading changes while additional recurrent connections render the locust circuit more tolerant to noise," and "the locust ring attractor is more robust to both types of structural noise." The fly uses global inhibition (faster, less stable); the locust uses local inhibition plus extra P-EN→E-PG recurrency (slower, more noise-tolerant). Both reduce to an 8-fold-symmetric (8-column) discrete ring. **Design implication:** a discrete N-state heading hold is a legitimate, biologically-paralleled object; it just buys robustness at the cost of resolution and true continuity — which is exactly the trade-off you want players to feel.

### B3. Alternative / non-attractor models of heading and PI

There ARE published models that approximate heading/PI without a canonical ring attractor — each buys something and sacrifices something:
- **Oscillatory interference (OI) models** (Burgess, Barry & O'Keefe, 2007; Burgess, 2008, *Hippocampus* 18:1157–1174; Hasselmo, 2008): distance along a preferred direction is encoded by the **phase** of a velocity-controlled oscillator (VCO) relative to a baseline oscillator; grid/PI emerges from interference. **What it buys:** integration without a tuned recurrent excitatory ring — just oscillators. **What it sacrifices:** it depends critically on *fine phase/timing* and matched oscillator frequencies, and phase noise accumulates. **Crucially for *Umwelt*: OI is ruled out by your sub-tick, no-fine-timing constraint** — it lives in exactly the timing-dependent regime you've excluded. Worth noting in the report precisely because it is the main non-attractor alternative and it is unavailable to you.
- **Leaky-integrator + population-coding PI** (e.g., the polar-coordinate leaky integrator of Merkle, Rost & Alt, 2006, *J Theor Biol* 240:385–399; the population-coding direction model with a low-pass-filter leak): a circular array of direction units, each a leaky integrator, accumulates displacement; the leak is a first-order low-pass filter (trivially a single primitive). **What it buys:** dead-simple, maps to your primitives, naturally reproduces ant distance-underestimation and error growth. **What it sacrifices:** it leaks (drifts/decays) and needs the heading supplied externally — i.e. it is "PI with forgetting." This is the model you should base F-PI on.
- **Cross-inhibition ("moving hill") without recurrent excitation** (Song & Wang, 2005, *J Neurosci* 25:1002–1014): a heading bump maintained by mutual inhibition rather than recurrent excitation. **What it buys:** no recurrent-excitation fine-tuning. **What it sacrifices:** still needs structured inhibitory tuning to avoid drift, and still faces the marginal-stability problem. This is the closest published thing to "an attractor from inhibition," and it is worth flagging as the most charitable case for what your mutual-inhibition primitive might approximate.
- **Vector-sum / sinusoidal-array accumulators** (Stone et al.'s mathematical framework; the sum-of-sinusoids identity): two sinusoids of the same period sum to another sinusoid whose phase/amplitude is the vector sum — so a population of cosine-tuned accumulators *is* a vector accumulator. **What it buys:** feedforward vector arithmetic, fully buildable. **What it sacrifices:** the *heading* feeding it still has to come from somewhere (the compass), and persistence still requires non-decaying accumulation.

### B4. View-based homing: the feedforward-friendly big win

This is where you give the player a real triumph. The mushroom body (MB) familiarity model (Ardin, Peng, Mangan, Lagogiannis & Webb, 2016, *PLOS Comput Biol* 12(2):e1004683, "Using an Insect Mushroom Body Circuit to Encode Route Memory in Complex Natural Environments") works like this: a visual scene activates projection neurons → a large layer of **20,000 Kenyon cells (KCs)**, each receiving input from 10 randomly selected visual projection neurons and exceeding firing threshold "only for coincident activation from several vPNs, thus images are encoded as a sparse pattern of KC activation" → all KCs converge on a **single extrinsic/output neuron (EN/MBON)** → during learning, coincident KC activity + a reward/modulator signal **depresses** the active KC→EN synapses. After training, familiar views drive few EN spikes (low novelty); the agent scans and moves in the least-novel (most familiar) direction. Abstracting the circuit's one-shot sparse-code learning, Ardin et al. estimate "the theoretical storage capacity of the ant mushroom body … at hundreds of independent images."

**Why this is more tractable than PI:** (i) it is feedforward — no attractor, no persistence-through-darkness requirement; (ii) it needs only ONE plastic layer with ONE kind of plasticity; (iii) the readout is a simple scalar novelty/familiarity that drives steering. It maps almost one-to-one onto your primitives: **sensor → excitatory interneurons (sparse-coding KCs via high threshold = a coincidence/AND-like nonlinearity, which is fine because it's a *magnitude* threshold, not fine timing) → a modulator-gated plastic edge onto a single output interneuron → motor.**

**The plasticity maps onto your modulator-gated per-edge rule beautifully — and honestly.** Hige et al. (2015, *Neuron* 88:985–998) showed KC→MBON plasticity is **presynaptic, dopamine(DAN)-gated, and depression-dominant**: pairing KC activity with dopaminergic-neuron input produces long-term depression at those synapses (demonstrated in detail in the γ1 compartment). This is exactly a **modulator-gated, depression-dominant, per-edge plasticity rule** — precisely your modulator primitive gating a per-edge weight change. So your plasticity primitive is most honest if it is **depression-dominant and modulator-gated**, not Hebbian potentiation. (Note recent nuance: plasticity can be heterogeneous and even bidirectional across boutons/compartments — e.g., appetitive potentiation in some γ compartments — but depression under dopamine is the well-established default.)

**Steering from familiarity — the lateralized accumulator.** Wystrach, Le Möel and colleagues (the "lateralised design," 2020; Le Möel & Wystrach, 2020, *PLOS Comput Biol* 16(2):e1007631, "Opponent processes in visual memories") show two MBs (left/right) can produce left/right familiarity signals that, compared as an opponent/bilateral difference, give a steering command independent of overall familiarity level — i.e., a **tropotaxis** on familiarity. This is again a pure feedforward bilateral difference (your CPU1-like steering primitive). The rIDF (rotational image-difference function; Zeil et al., 2003, *J Opt Soc Am A* 20:450–469; Stürzl & Zeil) is the underlying principle: panoramic image difference is minimized when the agent is aligned with the stored view, so "turn toward the rotation that minimizes image difference" is a complete, attractor-free steering law.

### B5. Honest "PI with forgetting" and the two-system redundancy

**Leaky PI and biological error.** As established (A2.2): biological PI is leaky and error-accumulating. Müller & Wehner (1994) "hidden spiral"; Merkle, Knaden & Wehner (2006) showed search width scales with run length (confidence degrades with distance); Sommer & Wehner (2004) showed systematic distance underestimation growing with distance. **"Repeated training does not improve the path integrator"** (Grah & Wehner; the integrator itself doesn't learn its way out of error) — so the error is intrinsic, not a learning deficit. This strongly supports modeling F-PI as a leaky accumulator whose home vector decays/drifts and "runs out," at which point a **systematic search** program (buildable as a half-center oscillator producing expanding loops centred on the PI origin) takes over.

**The two-system redundancy and how it corrects PI.** Ants run PI and view-based navigation in parallel and **weight them by reliability** (Wystrach, Mangan & Webb, 2015, *Proc R Soc B* 282:20151484, "Optimal cue integration in ants"; Hoinville & Wehner, 2018, *PNAS* 115:2824–2829, "Optimal multiguidance integration in insect navigation"). PI is weighted more heavily when the accumulated vector is long; view-based guidance dominates near familiar routes/goal. PI is also "uncorrectable" on its own (it just leaks), so views provide the drift correction — exactly the spatial-reset role that any leaky integrator needs (compare the grid-cell literature: "a spatial input is required to oppose drift in the grid representation"). Müller & Wehner (2010, *Current Biology* 20:1368–1371) framed PI as a **scaffold for landmark learning**: the home vector brings the ant close enough to acquire and later use views. **Design implication for *Umwelt*:** the honest, biologically-faithful architecture is *leaky PI that gets reset/corrected by feedforward view-familiarity near the goal* — and both halves are buildable from your primitives, with the un-buildable ring attractor sitting only inside the heading input to PI. This lets you ship a complete, satisfying navigation loop where the "wall" is contained, visible, and motivated.

### Taxa & honesty ledger (provenance of every mechanistic claim)
- **Ring attractor / continuous heading (E-PG, P-EN, copy-and-shift):** *Drosophila* (Seelig & Jayaraman 2015; Kim et al. 2017; Green et al. 2017; Turner-Evans et al. 2017, 2020). **Fly, not ant.** Locust comparison: Pisokas, Heinze & Webb 2020 (**locust + fly**).
- **CX path-integration circuit (TB1/TN/CPU4/CPU1):** modeled in **bee** (*Megalopta genalis/centralis*, *Bombus terrestris*) with electrophysiology + block-face EM (Stone et al. 2017). Connectome priors are **fly** (Hulse et al. 2021; Scheffer et al. 2020). **Not directly ant** — the ant CX circuit is inferred, not mapped.
- **Polarization compass / DRA / POL-neurons:** strongest data in **locust and cricket** (Labhart; Sakura, Lambrinos & Labhart 2008; Heinze & Homberg 2007; el Jundi); behavioral role confirmed in **desert ant and honeybee**. Polarized-light compass model: Gkanias et al. 2019 (cross-taxa, biologically constrained).
- **Mushroom-body familiarity / route memory:** model from **fly** olfactory MB, applied to **ant** (*Cataglyphis velox*) behavior (Ardin et al. 2016). KC→MBON plasticity: **fly** (Hige et al. 2015). Lateralized steering: ant-focused modeling (Wystrach / Le Möel 2020).
- **Behavioral PI, error, systematic search, cue integration:** **desert ants** *Cataglyphis* (Müller & Wehner 1994; Merkle et al. 2006; Sommer & Wehner 2004; Wystrach et al. 2015) and *Melophorus*; **well-characterized behaviorally, circuit inferred.**
- **Oscillatory-interference / grid-cell PI, fine-tuning, CAN theory:** **mammalian/general theory** (Burgess; Hasselmo; Seung 1996; Zhang 1996; Noorman et al. 2024) — applies by analogy; flagged as cross-taxa theory.

## Recommendations (staged)

1. **Ship raw polarization channels now.** Make instantaneous coarse heading decode (opponency + WTA/normalization) a solvable mid-game puzzle. Threshold to advance: player builds a population-vector readout that points correctly while the sky cue is visible. *Benchmark that would change this:* if playtesting shows the 180° ambiguity is too opaque, add an intensity/chromatic second channel rather than shipping a computed heading.
2. **Make view-based MB familiarity the feedforward triumph.** Implement KC sparse coding (high-threshold excitatory interneurons) + a single **modulator-gated, depression-dominant** plastic edge onto an output neuron + bilateral (left/right MB) difference for steering. This is the emotional high point where the player's honest circuit *just works*.
3. **Build F-PI as leaky PI explicitly labeled "PI with forgetting."** CPU4-style leaky-integrator bank (one per heading sector) + CPU1 bilateral-difference steering. Make the home vector visibly decay and "run out," triggering an emergent systematic-search oscillator. *Benchmark:* if players read the decay as a bug rather than a feature, surface a "confidence" meter (amplitude of the population code) that shrinks with distance — mirroring Gkanias confidence and ant search-width scaling.
4. **Let the wall stand. Teach it.** Let players attempt a discrete ring of flip-flops for heading-through-darkness and *experience* the documented failure modes (quantization, drift to nearest state, jumps, loss of slow turns). Frame the ring attractor's fine-tuning requirement as the explicit "why insects evolved a dedicated structure" lesson.
5. **Optional advanced content (only if you want a near-miss):** expose the **velocity copy-and-shift** (E-PG/P-EN-style) as a buildable mechanism, and let players discover it still drifts without a fine-tuned recurrent-excitation substrate to hold the bump. This is the most honest "almost works."
6. **Close the loop with cue-weighted redundancy.** Let leaky PI be reset/corrected by view-familiarity near the goal (optimal cue integration). This gives a complete navigation loop where the wall is contained inside the heading input only.

## Caveats
- **Provenance:** The mechanistic ring-attractor and plasticity data are predominantly **fly**; the PI circuit is **bee**; the rich PI/search/cue-weighting behavior is **ant**, but the ant *circuit* is largely inferred from fly/bee homology, not directly mapped. Every cross-taxa inference is flagged in the ledger above and should be treated as an estimate.
- **"Buildable" is a claim about computational class, not difficulty.** Sparse coding via a magnitude threshold is fine under your constraints (it is not fine-timing coincidence detection), but tuning a sparse code well enough for image discrimination is non-trivial — the *class* is feedforward-buildable; the *quality* depends on your sensor resolution.
- **The discrete-N robustness result is counterintuitive and parameter-dependent.** "Coarser can be more noise-robust" (Pisokas; energy-barrier intuition) holds against *drift*, but coarse rings sacrifice resolution and cannot integrate slow turns; within a fixed connectivity scheme, *bigger tuned* networks are more robust (Noorman). Don't over-generalize either way.
- **Oscillatory-interference PI is excluded by design** (sub-tick timing constraint), so the report treats it as off-limits despite being the main non-attractor alternative in the literature.
- **Time-compensation (ephemeris) is genuinely hard** and was treated only briefly; if menotaxis/PI over long in-game "days" matters, it deserves its own design pass.
